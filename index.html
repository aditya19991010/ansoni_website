About Me

From the marble city of Jabalpur, Madhya Pradesh, my journey into computational biology began with childhood experiences in healthcare settings. As the eldest son in a nuclear family, with my mother working as an Auxiliary Nurse Midwife and my father serving as a lawyer in civil court, I was exposed to both the healing arts and the pursuit of justice from an early age.

My fascination with medical machines like ECG, X-ray, and sphygmomanometers during hospital visits sparked my interest in the intersection of technology and healthcare. This curiosity evolved into a passion for using computational tools to improve healthcare accessibility and contribute to early disease prediction.

Guided by the principle of "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam" (the world is one family), I believe in harmonious relationships, respect for nature, and working for the betterment of society. My research focuses on modelling epigenetic drift to understand histone modification dynamics during aging, combining machine learning with biological insights.

Beyond research, I maintain strong personal values including regular donations to orphanages and schools on my birthdays, reflecting my commitment to giving back to society and following my mother's example of selfless service.
